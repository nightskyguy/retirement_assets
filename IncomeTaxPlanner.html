Income Tax Planner — How to Use

What questions does this answer?

A · Marginal sensitivity
How does my total tax burden change if my income is $10k higher or lower? The chart shows the full sweep so you can see cliffs, humps, and flat regions at a glance.
B · Sweet spots & danger zones
Where are the SS torpedo, IRMAA tier crossings, and NIIT threshold? The vertical reference lines and marginal rate curve (purple dashed) make them immediately visible.
C · True all-in effective rate
Federal + state + IRMAA combined, not just the marginal bracket. The white line on the Tax Rates chart is the number that actually matters for planning.
D · Withholding guidance
Hover your expected income on either chart. The tooltip shows each component's dollar amount and its share of total income — use this to set federal and state withholding.

How the income sweep works

The x-axis sweeps ordinary income from $0 to $1.1M in $10k steps. Ordinary income covers wages, IRA/401k withdrawals, pensions, and RMDs — anything taxed at regular rates. Social Security and capital gains are entered as fixed amounts that apply at every point across the sweep, letting you see how they interact with the income ramp.

The charts

  • Tax Rates — federal effective (blue), state effective (orange), total effective (white), and federal marginal on ordinary income + CG (purple dashed). Hover to see exact values.
  • Total Tax Dollars — stacked bars for Federal Ordinary, Federal CG, IRMAA, State Ordinary, and State CG. The teal line (right axis) is your MAGI — the figure used to determine next year's IRMAA tier.
  • Cross-chart linking — hovering either chart activates the tooltip on the other at the same income level.
  • IRMAA tier lines — click any IRMAA entry in the legend to toggle its vertical reference line on both charts simultaneously.

Caveats & known simplifications

  • IRMAA lookback: Medicare IRMAA uses your MAGI from two years prior. This tool applies it to current-year MAGI — convenient for planning but optimistic by about two years if you've recently had a high-income year.
  • OBBBA provisions (senior deduction, elevated SALT cap) are modeled from best-available bill text as of mid-2025. Phase-out thresholds ($150k/$75k AGI) and the $40k SALT cap are estimates — verify with IRS guidance when published. No automatic sunset is modeled.
  • Nebraska brackets are approximate based on the LB754 phase-down schedule; confirm with NE DOR for your specific year.
  • SS provisional income thresholds ($32k/$44k MFJ, $25k/$34k Single) are intentionally not inflation-adjusted — that is current law, and the reason the SS torpedo worsens over time.
  • NIIT threshold ($250k MFJ / $200k Single) is also not inflation-indexed — another orphan in the tax code, unchanged since 2013.
  • Excluded: AMT, self-employment tax, CA SDI, qualified dividends (modeled as CG), muni bond interest, CA personal exemption credits.
