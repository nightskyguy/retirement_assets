How to Use

  • 1. Profile & Ages: Enter the birth year and month of each person and estimated life expectancy. The current age and RMD start age are shown below each person's birth fields and update live. Use the Include Spouse checkbox to model a single taxpayer — unchecking it hides all spouse fields and excludes the spouse from the simulation. Retirement Start Age is the age at which you stop earning income; the simulation inflates tax brackets, SS COLA, and spending forward to that year automatically.
  • 2. Assets at Retirement Age: Enter balances in today's dollars. Brokerage Basis is used to calculate capital gains vs. principal. Total all after-tax investment assets into Brokerage, and all cash-like holdings into Cash. Should one spouse predecease the other, all IRA assets are inherited by the remaining spouse. Enter Roth balances separately — Your Roth IRA and Spouse Roth IRA are tracked independently throughout the simulation.
  • 3. Income (annual): Input annual Social Security and pension amounts. Set the start ages to see the impact of delaying benefits. SS amounts are adjusted annually by the CPI/COLA rate. Survivorship is the percentage of the pension that continues after You pass away.
  • 4. Assumptions: Dividend Rate and State Taxation are the most-changed inputs — set these first. Social Security Fail models the projected trust fund shortfall year and reduced payout. Growth is the annual return for IRA/Roth/Brokerage (note: dividend rate is additive — 6% growth + 1.5% dividends = 7.5% total). Cash Interest is for HYSA and money-market holdings. Inflation and CPI/COLA drive bracket indexing and SS cost-of-living adjustments. The collapsible Account Composition subsection records Equity/Bond ratios — informational only, does not affect the simulation.
  • Strategy: Set the After-Tax Spend goal (annual after-tax income needed) and Spend Delta (yearly change — default −1% per historical data).
    • Proportional Withdraw +% withdraws proportionally from all sources to meet the spend goal, then adds an IRA-only boost of 0–200% on top. At 0% it is the pure proportional baseline; higher percentages accelerate IRA drawdown, with surplus flowing to Roth via conversion.
    • Reduce IRA in N Years amortizes your IRA(s) to the IRA Goal balance over the chosen number of years. The Optimizer tests 2–25 years automatically.
    • Fill Federal Tax Bracket withdraws enough to fill the chosen bracket. If the spend goal requires more, spending takes priority.
    • IRMAA Tier ceilings withdraws up to the ceiling of the selected Medicare IRMAA surcharge tier, avoiding the next tier's higher premium.
    • ACA FPL cliffs targets income at 200/250/300/400% of the Federal Poverty Level to preserve ACA premium subsidy eligibility.
    • IRA Draw % withdraws a fixed percentage of the IRA balance each year (5–10% in the Optimizer).
    • Ordered withdraws from accounts in a fixed sequence until the spend goal is met: CBIR (Cash→Brokerage→IRA→Roth) depletes taxable accounts first to let tax-advantaged accounts grow; RIBC (Roth→IRA→Brokerage→Cash) depletes tax-free first; BIRC (Brokerage→IRA→Roth→Cash) depletes brokerage before tax-deferred. Useful for modeling specific depletion-order preferences.
    • The Optimizer always runs with Max Roth Conversion on — surplus after spending is converted to Roth. Enable it in the strategy panel to match the optimizer's assumption in single-scenario mode.
  • After Changes... clicking Annual Details, Chart, Optimizer, or Monte Carlo triggers a recalculation. On the Annual Details table, rows are highlighted yellow when filing status changes to Single; pink/red means the portfolio cannot cover the required draw. The Balances chart has a Both / Mine / Spouse toggle. The Optimizer sweeps all strategy variations and highlights the best result per column. The Monte Carlo tab runs all variations against stochastic return sequences and reports survival rates. Both tabs skip a full rebuild if inputs are unchanged.

For limitations and features not currently modeled, see the README.md.

Where is the Code

You can run this locally (download the *.html and *.js and *.css files from GitHub): github.com/nightskyguy/retirement_assets. Charts require loading remotely: cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/chart.js.

You can directly run the code from GitHub: tools.netcitizen.us/retirement_optimizer.html

Page loads are tracked anonymously using Google Analytics and Cloudflare Web Analytics. No personally identifiable information is collected; no account data you enter is ever sent anywhere — it stays entirely in your browser.
