Cumulative real purchasing power of $10,000 invested in a top-tier HYSA, adjusted for inflation and taxes.

Methodology: Each year, interest = HYSA rate × balance. After-tax interest = interest × (1 − tax rate). Inflation adjustment = balance × CPI rate. Net annual change = after-tax interest − inflation erosion. Cumulative balance starts at $10,000 and compounds year over year. "Uninvested cash" shows $10,000 eroded by inflation alone with no interest earned. "Roth" assumes 0% tax on interest (approximating tax-free growth).
HYSA rates: Top competitive rates estimated from FRED, FDIC data, and Fed funds rate history.
Inflation: BLS CPI-U annual averages.
Note: HYSA rates vary throughout the year; figures shown are annual averages of top offers. Inflation (CPI-U) also varies monthly; figures are calendar-year averages.
